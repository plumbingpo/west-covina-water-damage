How to Shut Off Your Water in an Emergency in West Covina, CA

Home / Blog / Finding Your Water Shutoff Valve in West Covina, CA

In a water damage emergency, the fastest way to minimize damage is to stop the flow of water. Knowing how to locate and operate your water shutoff valve could save you thousands of dollars and prevent extensive property damage. Every West Covina, CA homeowner should know their shutoff valve location and how to operate it before an emergency occurs.

Locating Your Main Water Shutoff Valve

In most West Covina homes, the main water shutoff valve is located in one of these areas: near the front of the house where the water line enters from the street, in the garage along an interior wall, in the basement or crawl space near the foundation wall, in a utility room or laundry area, or in a ground-level access box near the street (the meter shutoff). The valve is typically a gate valve (round handle) or a ball valve (lever handle). Ball valves are more reliable and can be turned off with a quarter turn.

How to Shut Off Water Quickly

For gate valves, turn the handle clockwise (right) several turns until it stops. For ball valves, turn the lever handle perpendicular to the pipe. If the valve is stuck, do not force it — you may break it and make the situation worse. In that case, shut off water at the meter using a meter key or adjustable wrench.

Individual Fixture Shutoffs

Most fixtures and appliances have their own shutoff valves that allow you to stop water to that specific location without shutting off the entire house. Look for shutoff valves under sinks, behind toilets, behind washing machines, and near water heaters. Knowing these locations allows you to stop localized leaks quickly while maintaining water service to the rest of your home.
